Acetic acid bacteria (family Acetobacteraceae) associate with insects

The family Acetobacteraceae (order Rhodospirillales, class Alphaproteobacteria) is a biotechnologically interesting group of bacteria , with a number of useful strains employed in the industrial production of vinegar and fermented foods, bacterial cellulose, and sorbose, a key precursor of vitamin C.

The family Acetobacteraceae consists of 46 genera and is organized into two groups: the acidophilic (32 genera) and the acetous (21 genera) clades.

The acidophilic is an heterogeneous group including acidophilic, neutrophilic, thermophilic and phototrophic bacteria. They are usually isolated from soils, sediments, paddy soils, acid or hot springs, sludge, sewage, freshwater ponds, air-conditioning systems, and certain Roseomonas strains have been isolated from human patients.

The genera of the acidophilic clade are: Acidibrevibacterium Muhadesi et al. 2019; Acidicaldus Johnson et al. 2006; Acidiphilium Harrison 1981; Acidisoma Belova et al. 2009; Acidisphaera Hiraishi et al. 2000; Acidocella Kishimoto et al. 1996; Acidomonas Urakami et al. 1989; Belnapia Reddy et al. 2006; Caldovatus Habib et al. 2017; Craurococcus Saitoh et al. 1998; Crenalkalicoccus Ming et al. 2016; Dankookia Kim et al. 2016; Elioraea Albuquerque et al. 2008; Falsiroseomonas Rai et al. 2022; Humitalea Margesin and Zhang 2013; Muricoccus Kämpfer et al. 2003; Neoroseomonas Rai et al. 2022; Novacetimonas Brandão et al. 2022; Paeniroseomonas Rai et al. 2022; Paracraurococcus Saitoh et al. 1998; Pararoseomonas Rai et al. 2022; Plastoroseomonas Rai et al. 2022; Pseudoroseomonas Rai et al. 2022; Rhodopila Imhoff et al. 1984; Rhodovarius Kämpfer et al. 2004; Rhodovastum Okamura et al. 2018; Roseicella Khan et al. 2019; Roseococcus Yurkov et al. 1994; Roseomonas Rihs et al. 1998; Rubritepida Alarico et al. 2002; Siccirubricoccus Yang et al. 2017; Teichococcus Kämpfer et al. 2003.
